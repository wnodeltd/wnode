# Governance and Economics

## Economic Overview
The wnode network is a decentralized compute fabric where value is created through the provisioning, execution, and consumption of compute workloads. Every node operator contributes real computational capacity to the global mesh, and the network monetizes this capacity by routing paid workloads to available nodes. Revenue flows directly to the community of node operators, who collectively form the economic backbone of the system.

The economic model is intentionally simple, transparent, and aligned with long term sustainability:

- Users pay for compute on a per job or per resource basis,
- Node operators earn the majority of revenue, proportional to the compute they provide,
- The network remains permissionless, allowing anyone to join, contribute, and earn,
- The steward receives a fixed fee for maintaining the network’s operational health.

This structure ensures that the network grows organically, rewards participation, and remains resilient and community driven.

## 100% Community Ownership
wnode is built on a foundational principle, the network belongs entirely to the community. Every node, every account, and every participant is an owner of a real, immutable digital asset. These assets are not symbolic, they represent:

- The right to contribute compute,
- The right to earn revenue,
- The right to participate in the network’s future evolution.

There is no central authority that owns the network. Instead, ownership is distributed across all node operators. This creates a naturally viral and self sustaining ecosystem where:

- Growth benefits everyone,
- New participants strengthen the network,
- Value flows outward, not upward.

Each account is a durable, transferable, and economically meaningful asset. Once created, it cannot be revoked or diluted. This ensures fairness, long term stability, and genuine community empowerment.

## The Steward Role and Operating License
wnode Ltd (UK) currently serves as the initial Operator and Steward of the network under a temporary operating license. This license is a commercial asset held by the steward, separate from community ownership of the network itself.

The operating license:

- Grants the steward the right to perform operational and maintenance duties for the network,
- Is owned by the steward as a sovereign commercial asset,
- Is not subject to community confiscation or revocation,
- May be retained, transferred, or sold by the steward, subject to applicable law and contractual arrangements.

The temporary nature of the wnode Ltd license reflects its role as a startup steward during the early stages of the network. Over time, the license may be held permanently by wnode Ltd, or transferred to another operator, potentially in a different jurisdiction.

In all cases:

- The license itself is an asset of the steward, not of the community,
- The network remains 100% community owned as a DePIN,
- The steward’s role is to manage and maintain the Mesh on behalf of the ecosystem, not to own it.

This separation ensures that:

- The DePIN remains sovereign and community owned,
- The stewardship function is professionally managed,
- The license can be treated as a real, transferable business asset without compromising community ownership.

## The Steward Fee
To perform stewardship duties, wnode Ltd receives a fixed 7% share of network revenue. This fee is:

- Fixed, it does not increase over time,
- Transparent, clearly documented and visible in dashboards and reporting,
- Purpose bound, intended to fund stewardship responsibilities.

Stewardship duties include:

- Maintaining the core codebase,
- Ensuring security updates and patches,
- Operating essential backend services,
- Providing documentation and support,
- Managing partnerships and integrations,
- Coordinating ecosystem and operational processes.

The steward fee is not a mechanism to claim ownership of the network. It is a service fee that enables the steward to:

- Keep the network secure and reliable,
- Invest in ongoing development and improvements,
- Support the community and ecosystem.

The remaining 93% of all value flows directly to the community of node operators and participants, preserving the core principle of community ownership.

## Authoritative Commission Splits

Revenue generated by compute jobs is distributed according to the following constitutionally locked percentages:

| Recipient | Percentage | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Operator** | **80%** | The node owner who provided the compute capacity. |
| **Steward** | **7%** | Fixed platform fee for maintenance and security. |
| **Founder** | **3%** | Permanent economic override for genesis founders. |
| **Affiliate L1** | **2%** | Direct inviter (parent) of the node operator. |
| **Affiliate L2** | **8%** | Secondary inviter (grandparent) of the node operator. |

These splits are hardcoded in the network's core logic and cannot be modified by the steward without a genesis-level constitutional update.

## The Affiliate Tree
Every node operator is automatically part of the network’s viral affiliate tree. This system rewards organic growth by allowing participants to earn a share of revenue associated with the nodes they introduce to the network.

Key principles:

- Every account has a parent (the inviter) and may have children (invitees),
- Revenue from compute jobs flows downward to nodes and upward through the tree according to predefined rules,
- The structure is infinite depth, allowing long term compounding of relationships,
- The system is non extractive, it does not reduce node earnings beyond what is transparently allocated for referrals.

This creates a natural, community driven growth engine where:

- Anyone can help expand the network,
- Everyone benefits from the network’s success,
- Growth is decentralized, viral, and self sustaining.

## Founder Allocations
At genesis, two permanent founder node slots were created. These slots:

- Are fixed and cannot be increased,
- Receive the same rights and revenue mechanics as any other node,
- Exist to acknowledge the initial creation, design, and bootstrapping of the network.

They do not grant special governance powers, override community ownership, or alter the economic balance of the system. Their purpose is historical and structural, not hierarchical.

## Future Governance Evolution
wnode is designed to evolve through community participation while respecting the separation between:

- Community ownership of the DePIN, and
- Commercial ownership of the stewardship license.

Over time, the community may influence:

- Protocol upgrades and feature roadmaps,
- Economic parameter adjustments within clearly defined bounds,
- Network expansion strategies and integrations,
- The expectations and standards applied to any steward operating under a license.

At the same time:

- The stewardship license remains a commercial asset of its holder,
- The network remains sovereign and community owned,
- Governance focuses on how the network evolves, not on expropriating private assets.

The long term vision is a mature, community anchored DePIN, where:

- The Mesh is owned by its participants,
- The steward, whoever holds the license, operates as a professional service provider,
- Both sides, community and steward, are aligned around transparency, reliability, and sustainable decentralized compute.
